IIM Kashipur · MBA Dissertation · 2022–2023

Antecedents and Consequences of Human–AI Relationships: Smart Voice Assistants

Surveyed 269 regular voice assistant users across Amazon Alexa, Apple Siri, Google Assistant, and Microsoft Cortana. Applied Relational Cohesion Theory (RCT), Exploratory Factor Analysis (EFA), Confirmatory Factor Analysis (CFA), and multiple linear regression to identify what drives user satisfaction, engagement, and brand loyalty with AI systems.

RCT Framework PCA + EFA + CFA Regression SPSS 269 Users 4 VA Platforms
The Autonomy Paradox
High autonomy in voice assistants drives engagement but reduces user satisfaction — users give AI the illusion of autonomy while subconsciously expecting it to validate their existing views.
Frequency Beats Intelligence
Interaction frequency predicted user engagement more strongly than perceived intelligence — habit and relationship-building trump raw AI capability in long-term adoption.
Brand Loyalty Blind Spot
None of the AI characteristics tested reliably predicted brand loyalty — AI product features alone don't retain users. The industry may be measuring the wrong success metrics.
Product Implication
Design AI products for human psychology as it actually is — not for benchmark performance. Familiarity and emotional comfort drive long-term adoption more than capability.
